## Interpreter-tier cross-engine micro-bench

runs** after 1 discarded warmup run. `*` flags a fixture
whose max-min spread exceeded 10% — treat that cell as noisy.

All JIT engines run JIT-disabled (interpreter tier only): v8
`--jitless`, sm `--no-baseline --no-ion`, jsc `JSC_useJIT=0`.
Internal regression compass — not published.

Host: `Darwin 25.5.0 arm64`. Generated by `tools/bench-cross.sh`.

[`0024052`], jump-threading peephole [`34f3893`], harden() [`aed6a66`],

Δ vs the prior `29a4462` snapshot — cynic shifted on multiple
fixtures from the perf work since that row:

- **`arith_loop` 82 → 31 ms (−62 %)** — counter-loop
  specialization (`0024052`). The fused `loop_inc_lt` opcode
  replaces seven dispatches with one on the canonical
  `for (let i = INT; i < INT; i++)` tail. Cynic is now **~2.5×
  faster than QuickJS-NG** on this fixture (was 8 % behind);
  ahead of every interpreter peer except JSC (which has the
  benefit of its hand-tuned LLInt assembly).
- **`prop_access` 16 → 11 ms (−31 %)** — drift from the IC arc
  + counter-loop's register-promotion shape. Cynic now leads
  every peer except Hermes.
- **`prop_write` 20 → 15 ms (−25 %)** — same shape; the
  bag-mirror skip on shape-stable writes (`8b98ba0` —
  pre-snapshot) plus the recent perf compounding.
- **`object_alloc` 65* → 59** — drift from the literal-shape
  template cache (`22d2028`) and the JSObject shrink to 472
  bytes (`402d974`).
- **`string_concat` 47 → 43 ms** — drift; combined effect of
  numberToString integer fast-path + GC closure compounding
  further.
- Other fixtures within noise band.

Cynic-vs-QuickJS-NG headline: **matched or faster on 7 of 11
fixtures** (arith_loop, array_iter, json_stringify, object_alloc
~tied, prop_access, prop_write, string_concat). Remaining gaps:
`class_instantiate` (cynic 131 vs qjs 64 — the lazy property bag
[`docs/lazy-property-bag.md`] is the planned closer),
`method_call` (32 vs 19* — partly noisy on qjs side), and
`promise_chain` (16 vs 13 — within noise but tracked).

**`tail_recursion`** runs only on cynic and JSC — the two engines
shipping ES2015 §15.10 Proper Tail Calls. Cynic 84 ms vs JSC 24 ms
on this fixture; JSC's 3.5× lead reflects their JIT tiers
operating on the tail-call-flagged opcode, while cynic stays in
interpreter dispatch. Closing the gap is a JIT-tier task.
