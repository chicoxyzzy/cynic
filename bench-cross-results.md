## Interpreter-tier cross-engine micro-bench

Subprocess wall-clock; times in **ms**, **median of N=5 timed
runs** after 1 discarded warmup run. `*` flags a fixture
whose max-min spread exceeded 10% — treat that cell as noisy.

All JIT engines run JIT-disabled (interpreter tier only): v8
`--jitless`, sm `--no-baseline --no-ion`, jsc `JSC_useJIT=0`.
Internal regression compass — not published.

Host: `Darwin 25.5.0 arm64`. Generated by `tools/bench-cross.sh`.

Snapshot at `29a4462` (merge of `gc-write-barrier-closure` —
write-barrier audit closure, 37 commits, every typed-slot setter
now routes through a barrier-aware helper).

Δ vs the prior `24f10be` snapshot:
- **`string_concat` −16 %** (56 → 47) — combined effect of
  `822b189` (numberToString integer fast-path) and the
  write-barrier closure merge. Now matches QuickJS-NG within
  noise (cynic 47 vs qjs 48).
- **`arith_loop` −3.5 %** (85 → 82) — typed-slot setters are
  measurably leaner than dispatch through the generic
  `Heap.writeBarrier`. Still 5 ms behind qjs's 77.
- **`object_alloc` +8 %** (64 → 69) — flagged noisy (111 %
  spread); needs more samples to be a real signal, but watch
  on the next snapshot. The 15 ms structural gap to qjs is
  the documented lazy-bag headroom (see
  [docs/lazy-property-bag.md](docs/lazy-property-bag.md)).
- Other fixtures: flat within noise.

Cynic-vs-QuickJS-NG headline: ahead on `array_iter` (22 vs 25),
`prop_access` (16 vs 17), `string_concat` (47 vs 48); matched
on `promise_chain` (14 vs 13*); behind on `arith_loop` (5 ms),
`prop_write` (3 ms), `object_alloc` (15 ms — noisy). The
remaining gaps all map to ROADMAP-tracked items.
